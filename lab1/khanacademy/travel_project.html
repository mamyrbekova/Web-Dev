Travel to Italy

  • Attractions:

    1. Colosseum

      2. The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an iconic ancient Roman amphitheater located in the center of Rome, Italy. It is considered one of the greatest works of Roman architecture and engineering.

    2. Cinque Terre

      3. Cinque Terre, which translates to "Five Lands" in Italian, refers to a picturesque coastal region along the Italian Riviera. It comprises five colorful and charming villages, perched on cliffs overlooking the Ligurian Sea

    3. Lake Resia

      4. Lake Resia, also known as Reschensee or Lago di Resia, is an artificial reservoir located in the Italian Alps, near the village of Resia in South Tyrol, northern Italy. The lake was created as a result of the construction of the Resia Reservoir in the 1950s for hydroelectric power generation.

    4. Leaning Tower of Pisa

      5. The Leaning Tower of Pisa, or simply the Tower of Pisa, is one of the most famous and recognizable landmarks in the world. It is located in the city of Pisa, Italy, in the Piazza dei Miracoli (Square of Miracles). The tower is part of a complex of buildings that includes the Cathedral (Duomo di Pisa) and the Baptistry.
